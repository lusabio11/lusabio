What is Plagiarsim?

PLAGIARISM is the act of using someone else's ideas or words without giving them credit. It can be intentional or unintentional, and can occur in academic, professional, or other settings.

University of the Philippines (UP) Students – 2019 Plagiarism: In 2019, two students from the University of the Philippines (UP) were involved in a plagiarism controversy after their thesis papers were found to have copied significant portions of content from published research articles and online sources without proper attribution. Consequences: The university investigated the matter, and the students faced academic sanctions. Nature of Punishment.

The nature of the punishment for plagiarism is multifaceted, reflecting the seriousness with which academic institutions, professional organizations, and legal systems view intellectual dishonesty. In educational settings, the immediate consequences often include failure on the assignment or course, with potential for academic probation or even expulsion, depending on the severity of the offense. Beyond academia, plagiarism can lead to significant professional repercussions, such as damage to one's reputation, loss of job opportunities, and legal consequences, including lawsuits or fines, particularly if the infringement involves copyrighted material. These punitive measures are designed not only to penalize the individual but also to uphold the integrity of intellectual work and encourage respect for original authorship..

Plagiarism Consequences

Plagiarism can lead to serious consequences, including academic penalties like failing grades, suspension, or expulsion, as well as damage to one’s reputation and credibility. In professional settings, it risks job loss, legal action, and financial penalties. It undermines personal integrity, stunts learning and creativity, and harms the value of genuine effort, affecting both the individual and the broader community.
